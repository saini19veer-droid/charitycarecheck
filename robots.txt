<h1>Hospital charity care in North Carolina</h1>
<p>North Carolina launched one of the most ambitious medical debt and charity care programs in the country. Every acute care hospital participates in the Healthcare Access and Stabilization Program (HASP), which requires free care, automatic eligibility for many patients, and has already erased billions in past medical debt.</p>
<div class="tag-row">
<a href="#threshold">Income thresholds</a>
<a href="#protections">HASP protections</a>
<a href="#find-policy">Find your policy</a>
</div>
</div>
<section class="section">
<div class="container has-rail">
<div class="prose">
<div class="card-grid">
<div class="card"><span class="mono-num">Program</span><h3>HASP (2024–ongoing)</h3><p>North Carolina tied enhanced Medicaid payments to hospitals agreeing to relieve past medical debt and adopt stronger, uniform charity care policies. Every eligible hospital signed on.</p></div>
<div class="card"><span class="mono-num">Free care</span><h3>Under 200% FPL</h3><p>Hospitals must provide 100% free care for patients at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.</p></div>
<div class="card"><span class="mono-num">Discounts</span><h3>Up to 300% FPL</h3><p>Substantial discounts (at least 50%) are required for patients between 200% and 300% FPL.</p></div>
<div class="card"><span class="mono-num">Applies to</span><h3>All acute care hospitals</h3><p>Unlike many states that only cover nonprofits, North Carolina’s requirements reach every acute care hospital in the state through HASP participation.</p></div>
</div>

<div id="threshold" class="section-head">
<span class="eyebrow">Income thresholds</span>
<h2>North Carolina’s charity care floors under HASP</h2>
</div>
<p>As a condition of receiving enhanced payments, all participating hospitals (essentially all acute care hospitals) must follow these minimums:</p>
<div class="table-wrap">
<table class="data-table">
<thead><tr><th>Income level</th><th>Required benefit</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td>Under 200% FPL (~$31,920 for 1 person)</td><td>100% free care</td></tr>
<tr><td>200%–300% FPL</td><td>At least 50% discount (sliding scale common)</td></tr>
<tr><td>Above 300% FPL</td><td>Hospital policy + any medical hardship provisions</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>
</div>
<p>For 2026, 200% FPL for a family of four is $66,000 and 300% FPL is $99,000. Many large systems go further than the state minimum. Hospitals must also use presumptive eligibility for patients already enrolled in Medicaid, CHIP, SNAP, WIC, or experiencing homelessness — meaning they can approve charity care without a full application in many cases.</p>

<div class="ad-slot ad-slot--incontent" aria-label="Advertisement"></div>

<div id="protections" class="section-head">
<span class="eyebrow">Key protections</span>
<h2>What North Carolina patients get under the new rules</h2>
</div>
<ul class="ledger-list">
<li>
<div>
<h4>Presumptive eligibility</h4>
<p>Patients on public benefits (Medicaid, SNAP, WIC, etc.) or experiencing homelessness must be automatically considered for charity care.</p>
</div>
<span class="tag">Required</span>
</li>
<li>
<div>
<h4>Free care at 200% FPL</h4>
<p>Higher free-care threshold than many states and the federal baseline many hospitals use.</p>
</div>
<span class="tag">Required</span>
</li>
<li>
<div>
<h4>Past debt relief</h4>
<p>Hospitals agreed to relieve large volumes of medical debt dating back years as part of the HASP bargain — billions have already been erased.</p>
</div>
<span class="tag">Historic</span>
</li>
<li>
<div>
<h4>No credit reporting of new medical debt (in practice)</h4>
<p>Participating hospitals strengthened processes and ended or sharply limited reporting of medical debt to credit agencies.</p>
</div>
<span class="tag">Protected</span>
</li>
<li>
<div>
<h4>240-day federal window still applies</h4>
<p>Nonprofit hospitals must still give you at least 240 days from the first post-discharge bill to apply under federal 501(r).</p>
</div>
<span class="tag">Federal right</span>
</li>
</ul>

<div class="callout">
<svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2"><circle cx="12" cy="12" r="10"/><path d="M12 8v4M12 16h.01"/></svg>
<div>
<h4>Medicaid expansion + HASP</h4>
<p>North Carolina expanded Medicaid. Always check Medicaid eligibility first — if you qualify, it usually covers more comprehensively than charity care. Hospitals are required to screen for public programs.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div id="find-policy" class="section-head">
<span class="eyebrow">Find your hospital’s policy</span>
<h2>How to look up a specific hospital’s rules</h2>
</div>
<p>Search the hospital name + “financial assistance policy” or “charity care application.” All hospitals must post their FAP. You can also contact the hospital billing or patient financial services department and ask for the charity care or financial assistance application. For statewide program details, visit the NCDHHS Medical Debt page (ncdhhs.gov/medicaldebt).</p>
