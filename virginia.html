State guide

Hospital charity care in Virginia

Virginia’s Certificate of Public Need (COPN) program requires hospitals that want to build or expand to commit to charity care. State law defines charity care as free services for patients at or below 200% of the federal poverty level. Federal 501(r) rules apply to all nonprofits as well.

Law

Certificate of Public Need (COPN)

Hospitals must demonstrate charity care commitments (including free care under 200% FPL) to obtain or maintain COPN approval for facilities and services.

Free care

Under 200% FPL

State definition of charity care for COPN purposes is free care for family income at or below 200% of the federal poverty level.

Discounts

Often higher

Many Virginia hospital systems (Inova, VCU, Sentara, Bon Secours, HCA Virginia, etc.) extend sliding-scale discounts well above 200% FPL in their own FAPs.

Federal floor

501(r) still applies

Nonprofit hospitals must follow the full federal suite: written FAP, 240-day window, AGB limit, and collections restrictions.

Income thresholds

Virginia free-care and typical discount ranges

Income levelTypical / required benefit
Under 200% FPL (~$31,920 for 1 / $66,000 for 4)Free care required for COPN hospitals; common free-care floor
200–400% FPLSliding-scale discount at most large systems
Above 400% FPLHospital-specific (hardship, uninsured discount, or payment plans)

For 2026, 200% FPL for a family of four is $66,000. Always download the hospital’s actual Financial Assistance Policy — many Virginia systems are more generous than the COPN minimum.

Key protections

What Virginia patients can count on

  • COPN free-care commitment

    Hospitals that hold or seek Certificates of Public Need must provide free care to patients ≤ 200% FPL as part of their charity care obligation.

    Required

  • Federal 501(r) full protections

    240-day application period, AGB pricing ceiling, and limits on extraordinary collection actions for nonprofit hospitals.

    Federal right

  • Medical debt interest / collection reforms

    Virginia has been active on medical debt protections (interest caps and other measures have been advanced or enacted in recent sessions).

    Protected

  • Plain-language policies

    Hospitals must make their FAP and plain-language summary easily available.

    Required

Medicaid & FAMIS first

Virginia expanded Medicaid. Check eligibility for Medicaid or FAMIS (children’s coverage) before or while applying for charity care — hospitals are required to help screen for public programs.

Find your hospital’s policy

How to look up a specific hospital’s rules

Search the hospital name + “financial assistance policy” or “charity care.” Major systems (Inova, VCU Health, Sentara, Bon Secours, HCA Virginia, Carilion, etc.) publish clear FAPs and applications. Call patient financial services and request the Financial Assistance Policy and application form.
