Northwell Health financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

Northwell Health is the largest private employer in New York State and operates 28 hospitals across the New York metro area — from Long Island to Staten Island to Westchester. Its 2026 financial assistance policy provides free care to patients under 200% FPL and sliding-scale discounts up to 500% FPL, with official income guidelines published annually. This guide covers what you need to know to apply at any Northwell facility.

28 hospitals

Largest NY private health system

Northwell operates 28 hospitals across Long Island, New York City, Westchester, and beyond — including North Shore University Hospital, Long Island Jewish Medical Center, Lenox Hill Hospital, and Staten Island University Hospital.

500% FPL discounts

Northwell's 500% FPL discount ceiling is above the state-mandated floor and among the highest of any major New York hospital system — meaning more families qualify for at least some assistance.

State program supplements

Northwell participates in New York's Indigent Care Pool, which reimburses hospitals for approved charity care and creates strong financial incentives to approve qualifying applications.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at Northwell Health

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before doing anything else, call Northwell Health Patient Financial Services at 1-800-995-5727 and request a fully itemized statement of every charge on your bill. Review it carefully — billing errors are common. Correcting errors before applying means charity care is applied to the correct baseline amount.

  2. Read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download and read Northwell Health's Financial Assistance Policy at northwell.edu/billing-and-insurance/financial-assistance-programs-policies. Pay particular attention to the income tiers, documentation requirements, and appeal process before starting the application.

  3. Complete the application

    Download the financial assistance application from the same page, or request a paper copy at any Northwell Health admissions or billing office. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for processing delays and initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Collect all documents listed in the section below. Make photocopies of everything before submitting. If mailing, use certified mail with return receipt to create a dated delivery record.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit your completed application and supporting documents. Follow up by phone after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt, get a case reference number, and ask about the expected processing timeline.

  6. Appeal if denied

    A denial is not final. Request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with additional or corrected documentation. Read our complete guide to appealing a denied charity care application for a step-by-step process.

Documents needed

What Northwell Health typically requires for a financial assistance application

  • Northwell Health Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months) for all household members
  • Government benefit award letters
  • Bank statements if self-employed
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter
