State guide

Hospital charity care in Arizona

Arizona does not have a statewide income threshold law that forces all hospitals to offer free care at a specific FPL percentage. Nonprofit hospitals must follow federal IRS 501(r) rules, and most large systems (Banner, Dignity / CommonSpirit, HonorHealth, Mayo, etc.) publish clear FAPs with free care typically in the 100–200% FPL range and discounts higher.

State law

No FPL mandate

Arizona does not set a uniform free-care or discount threshold for all hospitals. Federal 501(r) is the main legal floor for nonprofits.

Typical free care

100–200% FPL

Most major Arizona hospital systems offer full free care somewhere in the 100–200% FPL range in their published FAPs.

Discounts

Often to 300–400% FPL

Sliding-scale discounts commonly extend to 300% or 400% FPL at large systems.

AHCCCS first

Arizona Medicaid

Always check AHCCCS eligibility — it is usually more comprehensive than charity care for those who qualify.

Income thresholds

What to expect from Arizona hospital FAPs

Typical hospital range (examples)Common benefit
Under 100–150% FPLOften 100% free care
150–200% FPLFree or deep discount at many systems
200–400% FPLSliding-scale discount (varies by hospital)
Above hospital cutoffUninsured discount, payment plans, or hardship review

For 2026, 100% FPL is $15,960 (1 person) / $33,000 (family of 4); 200% FPL is roughly $31,920 / $66,000. Because there is no state floor, the hospital’s own Financial Assistance Policy is decisive — always download it for the facility that treated you.

Key protections

What Arizona patients can rely on

  • Federal 501(r) full suite

    Written FAP, plain-language summary, free copies, 240-day application period from first post-discharge bill, AGB pricing limit, and reasonable efforts before extraordinary collection actions.

    Federal right

  • No additional state FPL mandate

    Unlike California, Colorado, or North Carolina, Arizona does not force a specific free-care percentage on all hospitals.

    Important

  • AHCCCS (Medicaid) screening

    Hospitals should help patients check for AHCCCS eligibility. If you qualify, it is usually better than charity care alone.

    Best first step

  • Public posting of FAP

    Nonprofit hospitals must widely publicize their Financial Assistance Policy on their website and at the facility.

    Required

Recent state medical debt efforts

Arizona has pursued medical debt relief initiatives (including large-scale debt purchases). Charity care remains the primary hospital-level tool for current bills. Check current governor and AHCCCS resources for any new programs.

Find your hospital’s policy

How to look up a specific hospital’s rules

Search the hospital name + “financial assistance policy” or “charity care.” Banner Health, Dignity Health / CommonSpirit, HonorHealth, Mayo Clinic Arizona, Northern Arizona Healthcare, and others post clear FAPs and applications. Call the billing or patient financial services department and request the Financial Assistance Policy and application form — they are free.
