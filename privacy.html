State guide

Hospital charity care in New York

New York combines the federal 501(r) floor with a state-funded Indigent Care Pool that reimburses hospitals for uncompensated care, creating strong incentives to approve charity care applications — and strong legal protections while your application is pending.

Income thresholds Collection protections Find your hospital's policy
Program

Indigent Care Pool

New York's state-funded Indigent Care Pool (ICP) supplements hospital charity care by reimbursing hospitals for a share of uncompensated care costs — giving hospitals a financial incentive to approve applications.

Threshold

Up to 300% FPL

Hospitals receiving ICP funds must provide free or reduced care to patients under 300% of FPL, with specific discount tiers varying by hospital policy.

Law

NY Public Health Law §2807-k

Establishes the Indigent Care Pool and the obligation for participating hospitals to maintain formal charity care programs open to qualifying patients.

Applies to

ICP-participating hospitals

Virtually all general hospitals in New York participate in the Indigent Care Pool, making these protections broadly applicable across the state.

Income thresholds

Who qualifies for free or reduced care in New York

New York's Indigent Care Pool requires participating hospitals to provide free or reduced-cost care based on income relative to the federal poverty level. While specific tiers vary by hospital, the general statewide framework looks like this:

Income levelTypical benefit
Under 100% FPL ($15,960 for 1 person)100% free care
100%–200% FPLSubstantial discount (often 75–100%)
200%–300% FPLSliding scale discount
Above 300% FPLDepends on individual hospital policy

Many large New York hospital systems — including NYC Health + Hospitals, Northwell Health, and NYU Langone — go beyond the state minimum and extend charity care or sliding-scale discounts to patients earning up to 400% FPL or more. Always check the specific hospital's published Financial Assistance Policy for exact tiers.

Patient protections

Collection protections while your application is pending

New York has strong protections against aggressive collection activity while a charity care application is under review. Key protections include:

  • Collections must pause

    Hospitals must halt collection efforts — including sending accounts to collection agencies — while a charity care application is actively being reviewed.

    Protected

  • Credit reporting hold

    Adverse credit reporting is prohibited while an application is pending, giving you time to apply without immediate damage to your credit score.

    Protected

  • 240-day federal window

    On top of state rules, the federal 501(r) requirement gives nonprofit hospital patients 240 days from the first bill to submit a charity care application.

    Federal right

  • Notification required

    New York hospitals must notify patients of their right to apply for financial assistance at admission and on billing statements.

    Required

NYC Health + Hospitals

If you were treated at a New York City public hospital (Bellevue, Elmhurst, Kings County, etc.), NYC Health + Hospitals runs its own "Options" financial assistance program that extends free care to patients under 400% FPL — one of the most generous public hospital programs in the country.

Find your hospital's policy

How to look up a specific hospital's rules

The New York State Department of Health maintains oversight of hospital financial assistance programs. To find your specific hospital's policy: search the hospital's name plus "financial assistance policy" on Google — nonprofit hospitals are federally required to post this document publicly. For NYC public hospitals, visit nychealthandhospitals.org directly. You can also call the hospital's billing department and ask specifically for the "Financial Assistance Policy" or "Charity Care Application" — they are required to provide it.
