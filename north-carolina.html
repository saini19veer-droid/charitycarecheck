Hospital charity care in Massachusetts

Massachusetts has some of the strongest charity care laws in the country, combining federal 501(r) requirements with a state-funded Health Safety Net program that supplements hospital charity care and requires hospitals to proactively screen all patients — not just those who ask.

Income thresholds Health Safety Net Find your policy
Program

Health Safety Net (HSN)

Massachusetts's Health Safety Net reimburses hospitals and community health centers for services provided to low-income uninsured and underinsured residents, supplementing standard charity care programs.

Threshold

Up to 400% FPL

The HSN covers patients at or below 400% of the federal poverty level, with different benefit tiers depending on income and insurance status.

Key rule

Proactive screening required

Massachusetts hospitals must screen all patients for eligibility for public programs and financial assistance — you don't have to know to ask.

Applies to

All acute care hospitals

All licensed acute care hospitals in Massachusetts must participate in the Health Safety Net program.

Income thresholds

Who qualifies for free or reduced care in Massachusetts

Massachusetts uses both the federal 501(r) framework and the state Health Safety Net to provide layered protections. The HSN has its own income tiers:

Income levelTypical benefit
Under 100% FPL ($15,960 for 1 person)Full coverage through HSN
100%–300% FPLSliding-scale HSN coverage
300%–400% FPLPartial assistance, varies by hospital
Above 400% FPLDepends on individual hospital policy

The HSN is separate from — and in addition to — each hospital's own charity care program under 501(r). Patients may qualify for both, and hospitals are required to apply whichever benefit is more favorable to the patient.

Health Safety Net

How the Health Safety Net works alongside hospital charity care

  • Proactive patient screening

    Hospitals must screen every patient for eligibility for MassHealth, ConnectorCare, and the Health Safety Net — whether or not the patient asks.

    Required

  • Retroactive eligibility

    Patients can apply for HSN coverage retroactively for services already received, even after a bill has been issued.

    Protected

  • No immigration status barrier

    The Health Safety Net does not require immigration documentation for coverage — eligibility is based on income and Massachusetts residency.

    Protected

  • Appeal rights

    Patients have the right to appeal a denial of HSN benefits through the Health Safety Net Office.

    Your right

MassHealth first

Before applying for hospital charity care in Massachusetts, hospitals are required to screen you for MassHealth (Medicaid) eligibility. If you qualify for MassHealth, that covers your bills more comprehensively than charity care. The HSN then covers any remaining gaps for those who don't qualify for MassHealth.

Find your hospital's policy

How to look up a specific hospital's rules

The Massachusetts Health Safety Net Office, operated by the Health Policy Commission, oversees the program. You can find information at mass.gov/health-safety-net. For individual hospital charity care policies, search the hospital's name plus "financial assistance policy" — all nonprofit hospitals must post this publicly. You can also ask the hospital's financial counselor directly about both the hospital's own charity care program and HSN eligibility in a single conversation.
