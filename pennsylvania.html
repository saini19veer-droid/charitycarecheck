State guide

Hospital charity care in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania does not set a single statewide income cutoff the way California or New York do. Federal IRS 501(r) rules for nonprofit hospitals do most of the work, and large systems (UPMC, Penn Medicine, Temple, AHN, Geisinger) set their own — often generous — thresholds. Notification requirements and pending legislation are also important.

Income thresholds Key protections Find your policy
State law

Limited mandate

Pennsylvania requires hospitals to have a financial assistance policy but does not dictate specific FPL percentages for free or discounted care statewide.

Federal floor

IRS 501(r)

Every nonprofit hospital must maintain a written FAP, publicize it, limit charges to AGB for eligible patients, and follow the 240-day application window.

Large systems

Often generous

UPMC, Penn Medicine, Temple, and others frequently offer free care well above 200% FPL and discounts higher — always check the specific hospital.

Pending reform

Uniform application bills

Legislation has been introduced to standardize applications and improve transparency; watch for updates.

Income thresholds

What actually determines eligibility in Pennsylvania

Because there is no single state income mandate, your hospital’s published Financial Assistance Policy is the controlling document:

Typical hospital tier (examples)Common benefit
Under 200% FPL (~$31,920 for 1 / $66,000 for 4)Often 100% free care at many large systems
200–300% or 400% FPLSliding-scale discount (varies widely by hospital)
Medical hardship / catastrophicMany hospitals have additional pathways when bills are large relative to income
Above hospital cutoffUninsured discount, payment plans, or negotiation

Real-world variation is high: one hospital may give full free care at 300% FPL while another stops at 200% or requires a hardship showing. Always download the actual FAP for the facility that billed you.

Key protections

What patients can rely on in Pennsylvania

  • Federal 501(r) full suite

    Written FAP, plain-language summary, free copies, 240-day application period, AGB limit, and reasonable efforts before extraordinary collection actions.

    Federal right

  • Notification of assistance

    Hospitals (especially those receiving certain state funds) must notify patients of charity care options before aggressive collection.

    Required

  • No statewide income floor

    Unlike CA, IL, or NY, there is no universal FPL percentage every hospital must honor. Hospital policy rules.

    Important

  • Wage garnishment limits

    Pennsylvania has strong protections against excessive wage garnishment for medical debt in many cases.

    Protected

Check Medicaid and CHIP first

Pennsylvania expanded Medicaid. If you or your children may qualify, apply — it is usually better coverage than charity care alone. Hospitals must help screen for public programs.

Find your hospital’s policy

How to look up a specific hospital’s rules

Search “[hospital name] financial assistance policy” or “charity care.” Major systems post clear FAPs and applications (UPMC, Penn Medicine, Temple Health, AHN, Geisinger, WellSpan, etc.). Call the billing department and ask for the Financial Assistance Policy and application form — they are required to provide them free of charge.
