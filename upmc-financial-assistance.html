Stanford Health Care financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

Stanford Health Care — anchored by Stanford Hospital in the heart of Silicon Valley — is one of the most expensive hospitals in the United States. What most patients don't know is that it also has one of the most generous financial assistance programs in the country: completely free care for patients at or below 400% of the federal poverty level, with no asset testing under California law. This 2026 guide covers who qualifies, how to apply, and what makes Stanford's program distinctive.

Key facts

What you need to know about Stanford Health Care's financial assistance program

Free care to 400% FPL

One of the highest in the US

Stanford provides 100% free care — not just a discount — to patients at or below 400% FPL. For a family of four in 2026, that is $132,000 annual income. This is among the most generous free-care thresholds of any major US hospital.

California law

No asset test

California's Hospital Fair Billing Act prohibits Stanford from considering assets — savings accounts, home equity, investments — when evaluating eligibility. Only income counts.

Insured patients too

High out-of-pocket costs

Insured patients whose out-of-pocket costs exceed 10% of their annual income may qualify for financial assistance — even if their income is above 400% FPL.

Very high charges

Financial assistance is critical

Stanford is known for exceptionally high chargemaster rates. In documented cases, Stanford billed multiples of what insurers paid. For uninsured patients, the financial assistance program is the critical protection against devastating bills.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at Stanford Health Care

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before doing anything else, call Stanford Health Care Patient Financial Services at 1-650-736-1000 and request a fully itemized statement of every charge on your bill. Review it carefully — billing errors are common. Correcting errors before applying means charity care is applied to the correct baseline amount.

  2. Read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download and read Stanford Health Care's Financial Assistance Policy at stanfordhealthcare.org/patients-visitors/billing/financial-assistance.html. Pay particular attention to the income tiers, documentation requirements, and appeal process before starting the application.

  3. Complete the application

    Download the financial assistance application from the same page, or request a paper copy at any Stanford Health Care admissions or billing office. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for processing delays and initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Collect all documents listed in the section below. Make photocopies of everything before submitting. If mailing, use certified mail with return receipt to create a dated delivery record.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit your completed application and supporting documents. Follow up by phone after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt, get a case reference number, and ask about the expected processing timeline.

  6. Appeal if denied

    A denial is not final. Request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with additional or corrected documentation. Read our complete guide to appealing a denied charity care application for a step-by-step process.

Documents needed

What Stanford Health Care typically requires for a financial assistance application

  • Stanford Health Care Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months) for all household members
  • Medi-Cal determination letter if applicable
  • For insured patients: insurance Explanation of Benefits showing out-of-pocket costs
  • Government benefit award letters
  • Bank statements if self-employed (assets NOT required under CA law)
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter
What's unique about this hospital's policy

Stanford's charges are extremely high — financial assistance is essential

Stanford's financial assistance program is particularly important because Stanford is one of the most expensive hospitals in the country. Uninsured patients who pay full chargemaster rates can receive bills that are 3–5x what an insurer would pay for the same services. The financial assistance program completely eliminates this burden for patients under 400% FPL — making it essential to apply before making any payment toward a Stanford bill.
