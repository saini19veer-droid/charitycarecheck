State guide

Hospital charity care in Ohio

Ohio operates the Hospital Care Assurance Program (HCAP), a statewide free-care program for low-income residents who are not on Medicaid, layered on top of federal 501(r) requirements for nonprofit hospitals and individual hospital financial assistance policies.

Income thresholds Key protections Find your policy
Program

Hospital Care Assurance Program (HCAP)

Ohio’s HCAP provides free basic medically necessary hospital services to eligible residents at or below 100% of the federal poverty level who are not enrolled in Medicaid.

Free care

Under 100% FPL

HCAP requires free care for qualifying patients at or below 100% FPL. Many hospital FAPs go higher (often to 200–400% FPL).

Application window

Up to 3 years

For certain state-benefit hospitals, applications must be accepted for up to 3 years after notice. Federal 240-day window applies to nonprofits.

Applies to

Most hospitals

HCAP is a condition for many hospitals receiving state payments. Nonprofit hospitals also face full federal 501(r) rules.

Income thresholds

Who qualifies for free or discounted care in Ohio

Ohio has two overlapping layers:

Program / Income levelTypical benefit
HCAP — Under 100% FPL (~$15,960 for 1 person)100% free care (if not on Medicaid and meet residency rules)
Hospital FAP — Commonly 100–200% FPLFree or deep discount (varies by hospital)
Hospital FAP — Up to 300–400% FPLSliding-scale discount at many large systems (OhioHealth, Cleveland Clinic, etc.)
Above hospital thresholdsStandard policy + possible hardship review

For 2026, 100% FPL for a family of four is $33,000. Always check both HCAP eligibility and the specific hospital’s Financial Assistance Policy — many Ohio systems are more generous than the state HCAP floor.

Key protections

What Ohio law requires

  • HCAP free care mandate

    Hospitals participating in HCAP must provide free basic hospital-level services to eligible patients at or below 100% FPL.

    Required

  • Notification requirements

    Hospitals must post clear notices about free care and financial assistance in admissions, ER, and billing areas, and on paperwork.

    Required

  • Extended application window

    For certain programs, hospitals must accept applications for up to 3 years after sending notice with a bill.

    Protected

  • Federal 501(r) for nonprofits

    240-day application period, AGB pricing limit, and collections restrictions apply to all nonprofit hospitals.

    Federal right

Medicaid first

HCAP is only for people not enrolled in Medicaid. Always screen for Ohio Medicaid (and other public programs) before or while applying for charity care — hospitals can help with this.

Find your hospital’s policy

How to look up a specific hospital’s rules

Search the hospital name + “financial assistance policy” or “HCAP application.” Large systems such as OhioHealth, Cleveland Clinic, University Hospitals, and Premier Health publish clear FAP documents and applications online. You can also call patient financial services and ask for both the HCAP application and the hospital’s own charity care / financial assistance application.
