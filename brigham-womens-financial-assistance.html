Yale New Haven Hospital financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

Yale New Haven Hospital is the primary teaching hospital for Yale School of Medicine and Connecticut's largest hospital — and it maintains one of the most generous financial assistance programs of any major academic medical center in the Northeast. Free care extends to 235% FPL under Connecticut law, but Yale New Haven's own policy goes significantly further, with discounts extending to 550% FPL. Combined with Connecticut's landmark ban on medical debt appearing on credit reports, patients at Yale New Haven have exceptional protections. This 2026 guide covers everything you need to know.

Key facts

What you need to know about Yale New Haven Hospital's financial assistance program

550% FPL discounts

Among highest nationally

Yale New Haven's own policy extends meaningful discounts to 550% FPL — well above the Connecticut state minimum of 400% FPL. For a family of four, 550% FPL is approximately $181,500 in annual income.

CT credit ban

Medical debt off credit reports

Connecticut's Public Act 24-6 bans medical debt from consumer credit reports entirely — meaning a Yale New Haven bill cannot appear on your credit report regardless of the amount or outcome of your application.

235% FPL free care

State law minimum

Connecticut law requires free care for patients under 235% FPL — Yale New Haven meets and exceeds this floor.

Yale School of Medicine

Academic medical center

Yale New Haven is the primary teaching hospital for Yale School of Medicine, with 1,541+ licensed beds and expertise in complex and specialty care.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at Yale New Haven Hospital

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before doing anything else, call Yale New Haven Patient Financial Services at 1-203-688-2000 and request a fully itemized statement of every charge on your bill. Review it carefully — billing errors are common. Correcting errors before applying means charity care is applied to the correct baseline amount.

  2. Read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download and read Yale New Haven Hospital's Financial Assistance Policy at ynhh.org/about/financial-assistance.aspx. Pay particular attention to the income tiers, documentation requirements, and appeal process before starting the application.

  3. Complete the application

    Download the financial assistance application from the same page, or request a paper copy at any Yale New Haven Hospital admissions or billing office. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for processing delays and initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Collect all documents listed in the section below. Make photocopies of everything before submitting. If mailing, use certified mail with return receipt to create a dated delivery record.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit your completed application and supporting documents. Follow up by phone after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt, get a case reference number, and ask about the expected processing timeline.

  6. Appeal if denied

    A denial is not final. Request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with additional or corrected documentation. Read our complete guide to appealing a denied charity care application for a step-by-step process.

Documents needed

What Yale New Haven Hospital typically requires for a financial assistance application

  • Yale New Haven Hospital Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months) for all household members
  • HUSKY Health (Medicaid) determination letter if applicable
  • Government benefit award letters
  • Bank statements if self-employed
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter
What's unique about this hospital's policy

550% FPL discounts + CT medical debt credit ban = exceptional patient protection

Yale New Haven's 550% FPL discount ceiling is one of the highest of any major hospital in the country. For 2026, 550% FPL for a family of four is approximately $181,500 per year. A family earning $150,000 could still qualify for a meaningful discount on a large Yale New Haven bill. Combined with Connecticut's complete ban on medical debt credit reporting, patients have exceptional protections — even if their application is denied, the debt cannot hurt their credit score.
