UPMC (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center) financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

UPMC is one of the largest health systems in Pennsylvania and one of the largest nonprofit hospital systems in the country, operating 40+ hospitals. Its financial assistance policy provides completely free care to uninsured patients at or below 300% of the federal poverty level — above the 200% FPL baseline common at many hospitals — with sliding-scale discounts above that threshold. This 2026 guide covers the UPMC financial assistance program and how to apply at any UPMC facility.

Key facts

What you need to know about UPMC (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center)'s financial assistance program

Free under 300% FPL

Above national baseline

UPMC provides 100% free care to uninsured patients at or below 300% FPL — higher than the 200% FPL floor at many hospitals, extending free care to a broader income range.

Underinsured covered

Not just uninsured

Underinsured patients — those with insurance but significant remaining out-of-pocket costs — are also eligible for financial assistance under UPMC's policy.

40+ Pennsylvania hospitals

Systemwide policy

UPMC's financial assistance policy applies across its 40+ hospitals in Pennsylvania, including UPMC Presbyterian, UPMC Mercy, Magee-Womens, Children's Hospital of Pittsburgh, and regional facilities.

Presumptive eligibility

Faster determination

UPMC uses presumptive eligibility screening — a process that can determine financial assistance eligibility based on publicly available data without requiring a full application in some cases.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at UPMC (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center)

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before doing anything else, call UPMC Customer Service at 1-800-533-8762 and request a fully itemized statement of every charge on your bill. Review it carefully — billing errors are common. Correcting errors before applying means charity care is applied to the correct baseline amount.

  2. Read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download and read UPMC (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center)'s Financial Assistance Policy at upmc.com/patients-visitors/billing/financial-assistance. Pay particular attention to the income tiers, documentation requirements, and appeal process before starting the application.

  3. Complete the application

    Download the financial assistance application from the same page, or request a paper copy at any UPMC (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center) admissions or billing office. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for processing delays and initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Collect all documents listed in the section below. Make photocopies of everything before submitting. If mailing, use certified mail with return receipt to create a dated delivery record.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit your completed application and supporting documents. Follow up by phone after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt, get a case reference number, and ask about the expected processing timeline.

  6. Appeal if denied

    A denial is not final. Request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with additional or corrected documentation. Read our complete guide to appealing a denied charity care application for a step-by-step process.

Documents needed

What UPMC (University of Pittsburgh Medical Center) typically requires for a financial assistance application

  • UPMC Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months) for all household members
  • Government benefit award letters
  • Bank statements if self-employed or income is irregular
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter
What's unique about this hospital's policy

Presumptive eligibility screening available — may not need full documentation immediately

Pennsylvania does not have a state charity care law as strong as California, Illinois, or New York — so UPMC's 300% FPL free-care threshold reflects a voluntary commitment above the federal minimum. UPMC also uses presumptive eligibility screening, which means the hospital may be able to determine your eligibility based on public data without requiring you to gather extensive documentation first. Ask specifically about presumptive eligibility screening when you contact patient financial services.
