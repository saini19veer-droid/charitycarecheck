Massachusetts General Hospital financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH) is part of Mass General Brigham — one of the largest nonprofit hospital systems in the country and the largest in Massachusetts. As a Massachusetts hospital, MGH's financial assistance program works alongside the state's Health Safety Net (HSN) program, which provides layered protection for patients up to 400% FPL. This 2026 guide covers both the Mass General Brigham financial assistance program and the state HSN program.

Key facts

What you need to know about Massachusetts General Hospital's financial assistance program

Part of MGB

Mass General Brigham system

MGH is the flagship hospital of Mass General Brigham, which also includes Brigham and Women's Hospital, McLean, and others. The financial assistance policy applies across MGB facilities.

MA Health Safety Net

State program supplements

Massachusetts's Health Safety Net reimburses hospitals for uncompensated care for patients up to 400% FPL — giving MGH financial incentives to approve applications and providing layered protection.

Proactive screening

MA law requires it

Massachusetts law requires hospitals to proactively screen all patients for financial assistance eligibility — MGH must screen you, not just wait for you to ask.

Retroactive eligibility

Apply after the bill arrives

Like all nonprofit hospitals, MGH must accept financial assistance applications for at least 240 days from the first bill. You apply after receiving the bill, not in advance.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at Massachusetts General Hospital

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before applying, call MGH Patient Financial Services at 1-617-726-2227 and request a fully itemized statement of your charges. Review it carefully for errors — duplicate charges, services you didn't receive, or billing code mistakes are common. Correcting errors before applying means you're negotiating from the correct baseline.

  2. Find and read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download Massachusetts General Hospital's Financial Assistance Policy from massgeneral.org/billing/financial-assistance. Read the income tiers and documentation requirements before filling out the application — knowing exactly what's needed makes the process faster and reduces the chance of a denial for missing documentation.

  3. Complete the application

    Download or request the financial assistance application from the same page, or ask for it at any Massachusetts General Hospital admissions or billing office. The application is free. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for delays or initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Assemble all the documents listed in the section below. Keep copies of everything you submit. If submitting by mail, use certified mail with return receipt so you have proof of delivery and a submission date.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit the completed application with all supporting documents. Call 1-617-726-2227 after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt and get a case reference number. Ask how long processing typically takes and when you can expect a written determination.

  6. Appeal if denied

    If your application is denied, request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with any additional or corrected documentation. See our full guide to appealing a denied charity care application for step-by-step instructions.

Documents needed

What documents does Massachusetts General Hospital require?

Most Massachusetts General Hospital financial assistance applications require the following documentation. The hospital's current Financial Assistance Policy at massgeneral.org/billing/financial-assistance is the authoritative list — confirm requirements before submitting.

  • Mass General Brigham Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months)
  • MassHealth (Medicaid) denial letter if applicable
  • Government benefit award letters
  • Bank statements if self-employed
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter
What's unique about this hospital's policy

MGH must proactively screen you — MA law requires it

Massachusetts is one of only a few states that legally requires hospitals to proactively screen every patient for financial assistance eligibility — not just those who ask. If you were treated at MGH and were not screened or informed about financial assistance, the hospital may not have followed state law. You can still apply retroactively within the 240-day window.
