Cedars-Sinai Medical Center financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles is one of the most well-known hospitals in the country and one of the largest nonprofit hospitals in California. Under California's Hospital Fair Billing Act — one of the strongest hospital charity care laws in the country — Cedars-Sinai must provide free care to patients up to 350% FPL and meaningful discounts all the way to 600% FPL. This 2026 guide covers exactly what Cedars-Sinai offers and how to apply.

Key facts

What you need to know about Cedars-Sinai Medical Center's financial assistance program

CA Fair Billing Act

Stronger than federal floor

California's Hospital Fair Billing Act requires nonprofit hospitals to provide free care up to 350% FPL and discounts to 600% FPL — significantly more generous than the federal 501(r) baseline.

No asset test in CA

Income only

California law explicitly prohibits hospitals from considering assets (home, savings, investments) when evaluating charity care eligibility — only income counts.

Free threshold

350% FPL

Under California law, Cedars-Sinai must provide free care to patients at or below 350% FPL — substantially higher than the 200% FPL baseline in many other states.

Discount to 600% FPL

Broader than most states

Sliding-scale discounts extend to 600% FPL — for a family of four, that is approximately $198,000 in annual income in 2026.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before applying, call Cedars-Sinai Financial Counseling at 1-800-233-2771 and request a fully itemized statement of your charges. Review it carefully for errors — duplicate charges, services you didn't receive, or billing code mistakes are common. Correcting errors before applying means you're negotiating from the correct baseline.

  2. Find and read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download Cedars-Sinai Medical Center's Financial Assistance Policy from cedars-sinai.org/billing/financial-assistance.html. Read the income tiers and documentation requirements before filling out the application — knowing exactly what's needed makes the process faster and reduces the chance of a denial for missing documentation.

  3. Complete the application

    Download or request the financial assistance application from the same page, or ask for it at any Cedars-Sinai Medical Center admissions or billing office. The application is free. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for delays or initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Assemble all the documents listed in the section below. Keep copies of everything you submit. If submitting by mail, use certified mail with return receipt so you have proof of delivery and a submission date.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit the completed application with all supporting documents. Call 1-800-233-2771 after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt and get a case reference number. Ask how long processing typically takes and when you can expect a written determination.

  6. Appeal if denied

    If your application is denied, request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with any additional or corrected documentation. See our full guide to appealing a denied charity care application for step-by-step instructions.

Documents needed

What documents does Cedars-Sinai Medical Center require?

Most Cedars-Sinai Medical Center financial assistance applications require the following documentation. The hospital's current Financial Assistance Policy at cedars-sinai.org/billing/financial-assistance.html is the authoritative list — confirm requirements before submitting.

  • Cedars-Sinai Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months) for all household members
  • Government benefit award letters (Medi-Cal, Social Security, SNAP)
  • Bank statements if self-employed
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter (assets are NOT required under CA law)
What's unique about this hospital's policy

California bans asset testing — only income counts

California law explicitly bans asset testing for hospital charity care — Cedars-Sinai cannot consider your home value, savings account, or investment portfolio when evaluating your eligibility. Only your household income is considered. If a Cedars-Sinai billing representative mentions your assets as a reason for denial, cite California's Hospital Fair Billing Act.
