Brigham and Women's Hospital financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

Brigham and Women's Hospital is one of the most recognized academic medical centers in the country, affiliated with Harvard Medical School and part of the Mass General Brigham (MGB) system. Its financial assistance program operates under the MGB systemwide policy — providing free care to patients under 300% FPL and sliding-scale discounts up to 400% FPL — supplemented by Massachusetts's Health Safety Net program. This 2026 guide covers everything patients need to know before applying.

Key facts

What you need to know about Brigham and Women's Hospital's financial assistance program

MGB policy

Free care under 300% FPL

Brigham and Women's follows the Mass General Brigham systemwide policy — free care for patients up to 300% FPL, significantly above the 200% FPL baseline common at many hospitals.

MA Health Safety Net

Additional state coverage

Massachusetts's Health Safety Net program supplements MGB's financial assistance, providing additional coverage for patients up to 400% FPL who are Massachusetts residents.

Proactive screening

Required by MA law

Massachusetts law requires Brigham and Women's to proactively screen every patient for financial assistance eligibility — you don't have to know to ask.

Underinsured eligible

Not just uninsured

Both uninsured and underinsured patients may qualify. If your insurance leaves you with significant out-of-pocket costs, you may still be eligible for financial assistance.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at Brigham and Women's Hospital

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before doing anything else, call Brigham and Women's Patient Financial Services at 1-617-732-9677 and request a fully itemized statement of every charge on your bill. Review it carefully — billing errors are common. Correcting errors before applying means charity care is applied to the correct baseline amount.

  2. Read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download and read Brigham and Women's Hospital's Financial Assistance Policy at brighamandwomens.org/patients-and-families/billing-and-financial-assistance. Pay particular attention to the income tiers, documentation requirements, and appeal process before starting the application.

  3. Complete the application

    Download the financial assistance application from the same page, or request a paper copy at any Brigham and Women's Hospital admissions or billing office. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for processing delays and initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Collect all documents listed in the section below. Make photocopies of everything before submitting. If mailing, use certified mail with return receipt to create a dated delivery record.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit your completed application and supporting documents. Follow up by phone after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt, get a case reference number, and ask about the expected processing timeline.

  6. Appeal if denied

    A denial is not final. Request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with additional or corrected documentation. Read our complete guide to appealing a denied charity care application for a step-by-step process.

Documents needed

What Brigham and Women's Hospital typically requires for a financial assistance application

  • Mass General Brigham Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040) for all household members
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months)
  • MassHealth determination letter if applicable
  • Government benefit award letters (Social Security, SNAP, disability)
  • Bank statements if self-employed or income is irregular
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter
What's unique about this hospital's policy

Free care threshold is 300% FPL — higher than the national norm

Brigham and Women's 300% FPL free-care threshold is meaningfully higher than the national norm of 200% FPL. For 2026, 300% FPL is $47,880 for a single person and $99,000 for a family of four. Many families earning up to that amount qualify for completely free care — a fact that the billing department does not always volunteer. Ask specifically about the 300% FPL free-care threshold when you call.
