Houston Methodist Hospital financial assistance: the complete 2026 guide

Houston Methodist is one of the largest nonprofit hospital systems in Texas, operating 8 acute-care hospitals across Greater Houston. Its financial assistance program extends meaningfully above the standard federal baseline — providing free care under 200% FPL and sliding-scale discounts up to 500% FPL, along with 18-month interest-free payment plans for those who don't qualify for full assistance. This 2026 guide covers everything patients at any Houston Methodist facility need to know.

Key facts

What you need to know about Houston Methodist Hospital's financial assistance program

8 hospitals

All Greater Houston locations

Houston Methodist's financial assistance policy applies to all 8 acute-care hospitals in the system — Texas Medical Center, Sugar Land, San Jacinto, West, Clear Lake, Baytown, Willowbrook, and The Woodlands.

500% FPL discounts

Above typical threshold

Houston Methodist extends sliding-scale discounts to patients up to 500% FPL — notably higher than the 400% FPL ceiling at many competing hospitals.

18-month payment plans

Interest-free

Even patients above the discount threshold can access 18-month interest-free payment plans, reducing monthly obligations to manageable amounts.

Medicaid screening required

Apply first

Houston Methodist requires patients to complete Medicaid screening before or alongside the financial assistance application. Medicaid coverage would supersede the hospital's own program.

How to apply

How to apply for financial assistance at Houston Methodist Hospital

  1. Request an itemized bill first

    Before doing anything else, call Houston Methodist Financial Assistance Unit at 1-832-667-5900 and request a fully itemized statement of every charge on your bill. Review it carefully — billing errors are common. Correcting errors before applying means charity care is applied to the correct baseline amount.

  2. Read the Financial Assistance Policy

    Download and read Houston Methodist Hospital's Financial Assistance Policy at houstonmethodist.org/pay-your-bill/new-policies/. Pay particular attention to the income tiers, documentation requirements, and appeal process before starting the application.

  3. Complete the application

    Download the financial assistance application from the same page, or request a paper copy at any Houston Methodist Hospital admissions or billing office. Complete every section — incomplete applications are the most common reason for processing delays and initial denials.

  4. Gather your documentation

    Collect all documents listed in the section below. Make photocopies of everything before submitting. If mailing, use certified mail with return receipt to create a dated delivery record.

  5. Submit and follow up

    Submit your completed application and supporting documents. Follow up by phone after 2–3 weeks to confirm receipt, get a case reference number, and ask about the expected processing timeline.

  6. Appeal if denied

    A denial is not final. Request the specific reason in writing and submit a formal written appeal with additional or corrected documentation. Read our complete guide to appealing a denied charity care application for a step-by-step process.

Documents needed

What Houston Methodist Hospital typically requires for a financial assistance application

  • Houston Methodist Financial Assistance Application
  • Most recent federal tax return (Form 1040)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 2–3 months) for all household members
  • Medicaid screening documentation (required before FA determination)
  • Government benefit award letters
  • Bank statements if self-employed or income is irregular
  • If no income: signed self-attestation letter
What's unique about this hospital's policy

Texas has no state charity care law — Houston Methodist goes beyond the federal floor voluntarily

Texas has no state charity care law comparable to California, Illinois, or New York — which means Houston Methodist's policy is entirely voluntary above the federal 501(r) floor. The 500% FPL discount threshold and 18-month payment plans reflect a genuine institutional commitment to access, not a state legal mandate. This also means the policy could change — always confirm current thresholds directly with the hospital before applying.
